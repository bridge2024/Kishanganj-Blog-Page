About Kishanganj

Kishanganj is a district in the northeastern part of Bihar, bordering West Bengal and close to Nepal. It covers around 1,884 km² and (as per 2011 census) has a population of roughly 1.69 million. The district is noted for being the only tea-producing district in Bihar and has a mixed agricultural economy. (Sources are linked in the blog footer.)

Agriculture: Tea & Pineapple

Tea cultivation started expanding in Kishanganj in the 1990s; today several thousand hectares are under tea, with both smallholders and estate growers. The district also grows pineapple and other horticultural crops — pineapple orchards are locally important and contribute to farmers' incomes.

Why tea in Kishanganj?

The climate and soils in parts of Kishanganj (blocks such as Pothia and Thakurganj) are suitable for tea. Specialty gardens in the area have even won awards. Tea brings seasonal employment and has become a visible feature of the district landscape.

Quick facts

  • Area: ~1,884 km²
  • Population (2011): ~1.69 million
  • Literacy (district-level, 2011): ~57%
  • Languages: Surajpuri, Urdu, Hindi, Bengali and others
